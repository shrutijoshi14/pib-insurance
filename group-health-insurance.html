Group Health Insurance

Your employees are your most valuable asset — and when they or their families face a medical crisis, the last thing they should worry about is the bill. Group Health Insurance gives your entire workforce access to quality healthcare, from routine hospitalization to specialist procedures, under one policy designed for your business. It's not just a benefit — it's a statement about the kind of organization you are.

What's Covered

  • Hospitalization coverage: room rent, ICU charges, surgeries, diagnostics, and specialist consultations — all covered.
  • Family inclusion: extend coverage to employees' spouses, children, and parents under the same policy.
  • Cashless treatment: access a wide network of empanelled hospitals across India without paying out of pocket at the time of admission.
  • Maternity and wellness: optional add-ons for maternity benefits, dental, and preventive health check-ups.
  • Minimal waiting periods: employees are protected from day one — no extended waiting windows like individual retail plans.
  • Flexible coverage design: sum insured can be structured by employee grade, CTC slab, or applied uniformly across all staff.
  • Simple administration: one policy, one renewal, one claims interface — low overhead for your HR team.

Why It Matters

Lower cost than individual policies: group risk pooling brings premiums down significantly — better coverage for less.
Attract and retain talent: a strong health plan is consistently ranked among the top reasons employees choose and stay with an employer.
Tax advantage: premiums paid by the employer are fully deductible as a business expense.
Healthier, more productive teams: covered employees seek timely medical care rather than delaying treatment — reducing prolonged absences.
Less admin burden: one group policy replaces dozens of individual arrangements, freeing up HR time and reducing errors.

Who Should Consider This

Any organization with 10 or more employees looking to build a meaningful benefits program — whether you're a growing startup formalizing your HR structure, an SME competing for skilled talent, or a mid-market corporate standardizing employee welfare across locations.
