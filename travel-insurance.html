Travel Insurance

Travel opens up experiences — but it also introduces a specific set of risks that your regular health or life insurance doesn't cover. A medical emergency abroad, a cancelled flight, lost documents, or missing baggage can turn any trip into a financial headache. Travel Insurance covers the entire duration of your journey, so you can focus on where you're going rather than what could go wrong.

What's Covered

  • Emergency medical expenses: covers hospitalization, diagnostics, and medicines if you fall ill or meet with an accident while traveling abroad.
  • Medical evacuation: costs for transporting you to the nearest quality hospital or repatriating you back to India in an emergency.
  • Baggage loss and delay: compensation for lost or stolen luggage, and reimbursement for essential items if your bags are delayed.
  • Trip cancellation & interruption: recovers non-refundable booking costs if your trip is cut short or cancelled for covered reasons.
  • Loss of passport: covers the administrative costs of obtaining a duplicate or emergency travel document.
  • Personal liability: protection if you accidentally cause injury to a third party or damage property during your trip.
  • 24/7 global support: access to a worldwide helpline for medical referrals, legal assistance, and emergency coordination.

Why It Matters

International healthcare is expensive: a minor procedure in the USA or Europe can cost thousands of dollars — insurance ensures you get care without going into debt.
Help is just a call away: traveling to a country where you don't know the language or systems? Our 24/7 assistance team is your advocate on the ground.
Don't let baggage issues ruin your trip: compensation for lost or delayed bags means you can buy essentials and keep your holiday moving.
Visa compliance: for many countries (like the Schengen area), valid travel insurance is a mandatory requirement for your visa application.

Who Should Consider This

Solo travelers, families on holiday, students studying abroad, and frequent business travelers. Whether it's a 3-day domestic hop or a 3-month international tour, the risk of travel disruption is real.
