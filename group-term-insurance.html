Group Term Insurance

No employer can fully replace what a family loses when someone dies unexpectedly — but you can make sure they aren't left in financial ruin. Group Term Insurance provides a meaningful life cover payout to the family of any employee who passes away during their time with your organization. It's the most impactful welfare benefit you can offer, and at group rates, it's also one of the most affordable.

What's Covered

  • Life cover for every employee: a lump sum is paid directly to the employee's nominee in the event of death during the policy term.
  • Single master policy: your entire workforce is covered under one annual policy — straightforward to manage and renew.
  • Flexible sum insured: coverage can be a uniform amount, or tied to each employee's salary, role, or years of service.
  • No medical tests up to standard limits: enrolment is fast and paperwork-light for the majority of your workforce.
  • Voluntary top-up option: employees can choose to increase their own coverage by contributing an additional premium.
  • Optional riders: Accidental Death Benefit, Critical Illness Cover, Disability Benefit, and Terminal Illness Benefit.
  • Portability on exit: with select insurers, employees can convert to an individual plan when they leave the organization.

Why It Matters

Meaningful support for families: employees work with greater peace of mind knowing their family is protected if the worst happens.
Builds lasting loyalty: life cover is a benefit employees deeply value — it directly shapes how they feel about their employer.
Significantly cheaper than individual plans: the same sum insured costs far less under a group policy than a retail life cover.
Aids statutory compliance: in roles or sectors with welfare obligations, GTI helps meet those requirements efficiently.
Low administrative lift: annual renewal, simple additions for new joiners, and one clear policy document.

Who Should Consider This

Businesses of all sizes that want to give their employees' families real financial protection — from SMEs building a benefits package for the first time, to large corporates standardizing life cover across departments.
