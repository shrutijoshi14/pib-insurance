Property Insurance

Your premises, machinery, stock, and equipment are the physical foundation of your business. A fire, flood, earthquake, or large-scale theft can destroy years of investment in a matter of hours. Property Insurance ensures that the financial cost of rebuilding doesn't fall entirely on your business — and that you can get back to operating as quickly as possible.

What's Covered

  • Commercial Property: covers office buildings, factories, warehouses, and retail spaces — structure, fit-out, furniture, and equipment.
  • Industrial All Risk (IAR): comprehensive cover for large manufacturing operations — fire, machinery breakdown, burglary, and business interruption under one policy.
  • Shopkeeper's Policy: a packaged solution for retail businesses covering property, stock, cash in premises, liability, and employee risk.
  • Allied perils: earthquake, storm, cyclone, flood, lightning, riot, strike damage, and more — configurable by your location and risk exposure.
  • Machinery and equipment breakdown: covers repair or replacement of critical equipment — essential for tech-heavy or automated operations.
  • Business interruption cover: compensates for lost revenue and ongoing fixed costs while your premises are being restored.
  • Reinstatement value basis: insured at full replacement cost — not depreciated market value — so your payout covers what it actually costs to rebuild.

Why It Matters

Get back to business faster: insurance funding accelerates the restoration process rather than waiting for reserves to accumulate.
Protects your loan covenants: banks and lenders require property insurance on mortgaged or hypothecated assets — a lapse can trigger a default clause.
No depreciation gap: reinstatement value policies eliminate the underinsurance trap that leaves most businesses partially exposed at claim time.
Revenue protected, not just assets: business interruption cover ensures your income stream is preserved even while operations are paused.
One policy, multiple risks: fire, flood, theft, machinery — bundled under a single policy rather than patching together separate covers.

Who Should Consider This

Manufacturing units, warehousing and logistics companies, retail chains, IT parks, and hospitality businesses with significant fixed assets.
