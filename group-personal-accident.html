Group Personal Accident Insurance

Accidents don't follow a schedule — they happen on job sites, during commutes, and away from work entirely. Group Personal Accident Insurance ensures that when an employee is injured, disabled, or tragically killed in an accident, their family isn't left scrambling. It covers your entire workforce under one policy, 24 hours a day, anywhere in the world — and it costs a fraction of what equivalent individual policies would.

What's Covered

  • Accidental death benefit: 100% of the sum insured is paid directly to the employee's nominee.
  • Permanent Total Disability: full payout for irreversible disability — loss of both limbs, both eyes, or complete paralysis.
  • Permanent Partial Disability: scaled compensation based on severity — loss of one limb, one eye, or partial hearing.
  • Temporary Total Disability: weekly income replacement while the employee is unable to work during recovery.
  • 24/7 worldwide coverage: protection applies at all times — not just during work hours or on company premises.
  • Optional add-ons: medical expense reimbursement, funeral support, and children's education benefit for dependent children.
  • Flexible sum insured: coverage can be uniform across all staff or structured by role, grade, or salary band.

Why It Matters

Real protection when it matters most: your employees and their families receive financial support exactly when they need it, without delays or disputes.
Demonstrates genuine care: going beyond statutory requirements signals that your organization values its people — not just their output.
Cost-efficient at scale: group pricing makes comprehensive accident cover affordable even for businesses with tight HR budgets.
No individual underwriting: the entire team is enrolled quickly without medical tests or lengthy paperwork per employee.
Reduces your liability exposure: formal accident coverage shifts financial responsibility away from informal ex-gratia arrangements.

Who Should Consider This

Construction companies, logistics and transport businesses, manufacturing units, field sales teams, NGOs, educational institutions, and any organization where employees regularly work in or travel through higher-risk environments.
